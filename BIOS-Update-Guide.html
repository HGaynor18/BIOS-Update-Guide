How to Update Your Motherboard's BIOS

Why Update the BIOS?

Updating your motherboard’s BIOS can improve system stability, fix bugs, and add new features. However, it can also be risky if not done properly. Follow these steps carefully to avoid damaging your motherboard.

Step 1: Identify Your Motherboard and Current BIOS Version

Before downloading a BIOS update, you need to know your motherboard model and the current BIOS version.

  • Open a command prompt in Windows (search for cmd).
  • Type wmic baseboard get product,Manufacturer to find your motherboard model.
  • Type wmic bios get smbiosbiosversion to find your current BIOS version.

Step 2: Download the Latest BIOS Update

Motherboard manufacturer BIOS download Links

Go to the manufacturer’s website and find the support page for your motherboard. Download the latest BIOS version and any associated utilities. Make sure it matches your motherboard model exactly.

Important: Only download BIOS files from official manufacturer websites. Using incorrect or corrupted files can damage your motherboard.

Step 3: Prepare a USB Drive

To apply the BIOS update, you’ll need a USB flash drive formatted to FAT32. Follow these steps:

  • Insert the USB drive into your computer.
  • Right-click the drive in "This PC" and select Format.
  • Choose FAT32 and click Start.
  • Once formatted, copy the BIOS update files to the root of the USB drive (not inside any folders).

Step 4: Enter the BIOS Setup

Now, restart your computer and enter the BIOS setup by pressing the appropriate key during boot (usually Delete or F2).

  • Consult your motherboard manual for the specific key.
  • Once in the BIOS, navigate to the update section (often called EZ Flash, Q-Flash, or something similar).

Step 5: Update the BIOS

With the USB drive inserted, select the BIOS update option and follow the on-screen instructions.

  • Choose the BIOS file from the USB drive.
  • Confirm that you want to start the update process.
Warning: Do not turn off your computer or remove the USB drive during the update process. Doing so can brick your motherboard.

Step 6: Restart and Configure BIOS Settings

Once the update is complete, your computer will restart. Enter the BIOS again to reconfigure any settings that were reset during the update, such as boot order or overclocking profiles.
